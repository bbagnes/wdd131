Sádaba, Zaragoza, Aragon, Spain

Sádaba, is a small community in the Zaragoza region of Aragon, Spain. Known best for the Sádaba Castle, and Roman Mausoleum of the Atilii.+
Visible from miles around, Sádaba Castle is and ancient castle believed to have been built some time during the first half of the 13th century. It is a typical Middle Ages castle with Cistercian-style decorations, tall walls and seven square towers. It is unknown who built the castle, but it's believed that Sancho VII of Navarre ordered it to be built. Inside of the castle are the remains of two large halls, as well as a large cistern to store rain water, and a chapel.+
Little is known about the lives of Bagüés family when Joaquin resided in Sádaba. Around 1923 Joaquin's brother Hilario; who'd traveled with him when he moved to Mexico and the U.S.; returned to Sádaba to care for their ailing father.
